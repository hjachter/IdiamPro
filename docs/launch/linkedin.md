## Post (200-300 words)

After two years of nights and weekends, I'm launching SecondBrainWare today.

It's an AI-native outliner for your second brain — and it ships on macOS, iPhone, iPad, and the web from day one, on a single subscription.

I built it because I kept watching my best thinking disappear. The insight from a great Claude conversation, buried in chat history I'd never scroll back through. The highlight from a PDF, lost in a folder I never reopened. The shower-thought that never got written down because opening a note app and finding the right place to put it took longer than the thought lasted.

Most "AI note apps" are a traditional note app with a chat sidebar bolted on. SecondBrainWare is the opposite — AI is structural, not conversational. You capture in one keystroke from anywhere. The system silently tags and organizes. You can import a PDF, a YouTube transcript, or an exported AI chat and watch the content fold into your existing outline structure, not get dumped at the bottom.

A few principles that shaped the product:
- Privacy-first: no tracking, no data monetization, AI consent is opt-in
- Bring your own API key if you want full control over AI vendor costs
- One subscription across every platform — your second brain shouldn't fragment
- Cross-platform from day one (Mac, iOS, web) with Windows, Linux, and Android on the roadmap

It's free to try with no trial clock and no credit card. I'd be honored if you took a look — and even more honored if you told me where it falls short.
