# SecondBrainWare — Beta Tester Outreach

Goal: ~20 active beta testers via TestFlight before public launch. Mix of friends/colleagues, PKM-curious strangers, and a few power-user note-takers.

---

## 1. Beta Invite Email (Warm / Personal Network)

Use this for friends, ex-coworkers, and people you've talked notes with before. Keep the subject line low-key — this should feel like a one-to-one note, not a campaign.

**Subject:** Quick favor — would you try my app?

> Hey [Name],
>
> I've been heads-down for the past several months building something I actually want to use myself: a personal-knowledge app called SecondBrainWare. It's an outliner at heart — think old-school OmniOutliner — with an AI assistant that can summarize, expand, and help you think through a node. Local files, no lock-in, runs on Mac, iPhone, and the web.
>
> I'd love to put it in your hands before I launch publicly. I'm specifically hoping you'll:
>
> - Open it for 15 minutes, try capturing something real (a meeting note, a project plan, a reading list).
> - Tell me one thing that felt great and one thing that annoyed you.
> - Hit the in-app "Send Feedback" button when anything weird happens.
>
> Total time commitment: 30 minutes over the next two weeks. No obligation to keep using it after.
>
> If you're in, reply with your Apple ID email and I'll add you to TestFlight today.
>
> Thank you — this kind of early read is the most valuable thing anyone can give me right now.
>
> Howard

## 2. Recruiting Channels

Work through these in order. Stop adding testers once you have 20 active ones — too many is worse than too few.

### Tier 1 — Highest signal (start here)
- **Personal network.** Direct emails / DMs to 30-40 people. Expect ~40% conversion. This is the bulk of the cohort.
- **X / Twitter call-out.** One post from Howard's account: short, no hype, what it is, who it's for, link to a sign-up form. Pin for a week.
- **IndieHackers.** Post in the "Looking for Feedback" group with a screenshot and a link. Engage in comments — don't drop and run.

### Tier 2 — Niche communities
- **r/ObsidianMD** — Skeptical audience, but the right one. Post under "Tools & Resources" flair, lead with what's different from Obsidian (AI-native, cleaner outliner, hosted sync option). Do not pitch — describe.
- **r/PKMS** — Smaller but on-topic. Same approach.
- **r/productivity** — Broader, less targeted. Only if Tier 1 falls short.
- **r/Notion** — Tread carefully; mods are strict on self-promotion. Read the rules before posting.

### Tier 3 — Discord / chat communities
- **Linking Your Thinking Discord** (Nick Milo's community) — Lurk first, contribute for a week, then mention the beta in #show-and-tell.
- **Obsidian Members Discord** — Same playbook.
- **IndieHackers Discord** — Active "show off" channel for solo founders.
- **Tools for Thought** Discord / forums — TfT crowd loves new outliners.

### Tier 4 — Last resort
- Hacker News "Show HN" — Save this for the public launch, not the beta. Spending the HN card now wastes it.
- Product Hunt — Same. Save for launch day.

## 3. Beta Agreement (NDA-Lite)

Paste this into the TestFlight description and the welcome email. It's not a real NDA — it's a social contract.

> **Welcome to the SecondBrainWare Beta**
>
> A few quick things before you dive in:
>
> 1. **This is beta software.** It will crash. It will lose a node occasionally. Sync will sometimes do something weird. Please back up anything important — SecondBrainWare stores files locally on your device, so a Time Machine / iCloud backup is enough.
>
> 2. **Please don't publicly review or screenshot the app yet.** I'm targeting a public launch in [month]. Posting reviews, App Store-style takes, or marketing-quality screenshots before then will hurt the launch. After launch, please post away — I'd be grateful.
>
> 3. **Private sharing is fine and encouraged.** Show it to a friend, mention it in a DM, forward this email. Word-of-mouth from beta testers is gold; public posts before launch are not.
>
> 4. **Your feedback is the deal.** In exchange for early access, I'm asking for honest, specific feedback — even brutal feedback. The in-app "Send Feedback" button is the fastest path; you can also reply to any email from me.
>
> 5. **Your data is yours.** I have no analytics on your notes themselves. Crash reports and basic usage telemetry only.
>
> If any of that is a problem, no worries — just let me know and I'll remove you from the build.
>
> — Howard

## 4. Feedback Collection Process

Three channels, in order of preference:

1. **TestFlight in-app feedback** (screenshot + note attached automatically). Default channel. Tell testers this is where to send bugs.
2. **Dedicated email:** `beta@2ndbrainware.com` (or `howard@2ndbrainware.com` if a dedicated alias isn't set up yet). Used for longer-form thoughts.
3. **Optional Typeform / Google Form** — Send once at the start (intro questions: what PKM app are you using today, what do you want from this) and once at week three (what stuck, what didn't, would you pay $X/mo).

**Triage cadence:**
- Skim feedback daily, reply within 24 hours even if just "thanks, logged."
- Batch-process into the IdiamPro - Testing outline weekly.
- Anything that lands in "fix before launch" goes straight into TaskCreate.

**What to actively ask testers to try (so you get coverage, not just first-impression notes):**
- Capture something real on day one (a project plan or meeting notes).
- Use the AI assistant on a real node at least once.
- Try it on at least two devices (Mac + iPhone) and check that sync feels OK.
- Edit a node offline, come back online, confirm nothing was lost.

---

## 5. Cohort Composition Target

Aim for diversity so you find different bugs:

- 5 hardcore PKM users (current Obsidian / Logseq / Roam users)
- 5 "normies" who currently use Notes.app or Notion casually
- 3 writers / researchers
- 3 developers (they find weird edge cases)
- 2 students
- 2 people on older Macs / older iPhones

If the cohort is all power users, you'll over-index on advanced features and miss obvious onboarding holes.
