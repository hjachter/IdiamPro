# SecondBrainWare — Influencer & Newsletter Outreach

Goal: seed 5-10 reviews / mentions / demos that drop in launch week. Budget: $0. Currency: free Pro Lifetime, early access, founder Q&A access for their audience.

Timing: send first wave **2-4 weeks before public launch**. Reviews need time to be drafted; YouTubers shoot 1-2 weeks ahead.

---

## 2. Cold Outreach Email Template (under 150 words)

Subject lines that work: their name first, then specifics. Avoid "quick question" — they get 200 a day.

**Subject:** Loved your [piece title] — built something you might want to try

> Hi [Name],
>
> Your [recent video / newsletter / post] on [SPECIFIC TOPIC — e.g. "the case for outlines over docs" or "AI that actually helps you think"] hit me in exactly the place I've been working for the past year.
>
> I'm a solo founder, and I just finished SecondBrainWare — an outliner-first PKM app for Mac, iPhone, and the web. Local files, no lock-in, AI that operates *on* your nodes instead of replacing them.
>
> I'd love to get it in your hands two weeks before public launch. The offer:
>
> - Free Pro Lifetime, no strings.
> - Early access to a private build with the AI features unlocked.
> - Happy to do a 30-min Q&A with your audience (live or recorded) if it's useful.
>
> One question: would you want to take a look?
>
> No expectation of a review either way.
>
> Howard Jachter
> [link]

## 4. What to Offer (in order of value to them)

1. **Founder Q&A / AMA for their audience** — costs you nothing, gives them content. This is the single most useful thing you can offer a creator.
2. **Exclusive early demo / private build** — they get to be first. Tell them explicitly: "you have a two-week head start before anyone else sees this."
3. **Free Pro Lifetime** for them personally — table stakes.
4. **Promo codes / Pro Lifetime to give to their audience** — for newsletter writers especially. 10 codes is enough to make a giveaway segment.
5. **A bespoke feature demo recorded for them** — only if they ask.

**Do not offer:**
- Money. You don't have it, and offering small money cheapens the pitch.
- Affiliate links unless they ask. Most serious reviewers don't want them.

## 5. Outreach Timing & Cadence

- **T-minus 4 weeks:** First wave to Tier-A targets (MacStories, Nick Milo, Anne-Laure Le Cunff, Dan Shipper). These need the most lead time.
- **T-minus 3 weeks:** Second wave to indie-Mac reviewers and AI-tool newsletters.
- **T-minus 2 weeks:** Build-in-public X accounts. They move fast and don't need lead time.
- **T-minus 1 week:** Final reminder note to anyone who said "sure, send it." Light touch.
- **Launch day:** A short personal "we're live" note to everyone who got access — give them the public link to share.

## 6. Tracking Sheet (set up before sending)

Keep one simple sheet. Columns:

- Name / handle / outlet
- Category (A-E)
- Last relevant piece (URL)
- Date contacted
- Response (yes / no / silence / ask later)
- TestFlight added? (date)
- Mention / review posted? (URL + date)
- Notes

Don't over-engineer. A markdown table or a single Airtable view is enough.
