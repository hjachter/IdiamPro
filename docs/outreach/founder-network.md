# SecondBrainWare — Founder Personal Network Outreach

Goal: turn Howard's existing network into the first 50-100 install-day users, plus the source of the first wave of beta testers. This is *not* a marketing channel — it's a "ask people who know me to actually look at the thing I've been working on" channel.

The tone is one notch warmer and more vulnerable than the generic beta invite. These people are doing you a favor. Say so.

---

## 1. Email Template — Close Contacts

For people you'd email or text directly: family, close friends, ex-coworkers, mentors, anyone who has asked "what are you working on?" in the last year. One-to-one. Personalize the first line every single time.

**Subject:** What I've been heads-down on

> Hey [Name],
>
> [One personal sentence — reference last conversation, their kid's name, their recent job change, anything that proves this is not a blast email.]
>
> I want to tell you what I've been working on, because honestly I'd like your eyes on it before I show the world.
>
> It's called SecondBrainWare. It's a personal-knowledge app — an outliner with an AI assistant — that I've been building solo for the past several months. Mac, iPhone, and web. The idea is that the notes you take should be yours forever, stored as plain files on your computer, and the AI should help you think *with* them instead of replacing the thinking.
>
> Public launch is in a few weeks. Before then, I'd love to:
>
> 1. **Put it on your devices.** TestFlight on iPhone, a DMG on Mac. Takes 30 seconds.
> 2. **Get honest feedback.** Not "this is great, Howard" feedback — actual "this confused me / this annoyed me / I didn't get why this exists" feedback. That's the gift.
>
> No obligation to keep using it, and no obligation to post about it. If, after the launch, you feel like sharing it — that would mean the world. But the ask right now is just: take a look, tell me what you really think.
>
> Reply with the email tied to your Apple ID and I'll send you a build.
>
> Thanks for everything,
> Howard

## 4. Platforms / Communities to Post In

Personal account, personal voice, personal post — same content adapted to each platform's norms.

### Tier 1 — Highest yield for a solo founder
- **X / Twitter** — Personal account. Pin the launch post for a week. Reply to every single comment.
- **LinkedIn** — Surprisingly effective for "I built something" posts because the network is professional. Lead with the "year of solo work" angle, not the product features.
- **Personal email** — Send to anyone who's expressed interest over the past year. BCC for privacy.

### Tier 2 — Warm-network amplification
- **Facebook** — If Howard still uses it. Family + old friends = surprise sources of beta testers.
- **Instagram** — Story format works better than feed. A 15-second screen recording of the app + a link sticker.
- **Threads / Bluesky** — Cross-post the X content. Low effort, occasional unexpected reach.

### Tier 3 — Slack / Discord / private communities
- **Any Slack workspace Howard belongs to** (alumni, old jobs, professional groups) — Drop into #general or #show-and-tell with one personal sentence and a link. Check rules first.
- **Any Discord servers Howard is in** — Same.
- **WhatsApp / iMessage group chats** — The most personal channel of all. Tell each one in your own voice.

### Tier 4 — Local / offline
- **In-person mentions** — Anyone Howard sees this month. A line in conversation does more than a tweet.
- **Local meetups / professional groups** — If any are coming up in the launch window, bring it up.

---

## 5. What to Avoid

- **Mass-blast emails with mail merge.** People can smell them. One-to-one or one-to-five.
- **Asking people to "share" before they've used it.** They won't, and it'll feel transactional. Get them to try it first; the share comes naturally if they like it.
- **Apologizing for self-promotion.** "Sorry to bother you with this" undermines the work. Be direct, be warm, be brief.
- **Posting the same copy on every platform on the same day.** Stagger by a few hours and adjust voice per platform.

---

## 6. After Launch — The 24-Hour Loop

For the first 24 hours after the public post:

- Reply to every comment, DM, and email personally.
- Screenshot any thoughtful piece of feedback and use it (with permission) for the next day's posts.
- If someone says something nice publicly, thank them publicly. If they say something critical, thank them privately and ask follow-up questions.
- Track every install / signup that day. That number is the baseline for everything that follows.

---

## 7. The Honest Truth About This

The personal network is the only marketing channel a solo founder with zero budget can fully control. It will not scale, but it will be the source of your first 100 real users, your first 10 testimonials, and your first 3 paying customers. Treat each of those conversations like it matters — because it does.
